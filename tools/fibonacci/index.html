Sunflower Seeds

Sunflower seed heads arrange in spirals of Fibonacci numbers (typically 34 and 55) to pack seeds as efficiently as possible.

Tree Branching

Many trees exhibit Fibonacci branching patterns. Each branch splits into two, and the number of branches at each level follows the sequence.

Nautilus Shell

The nautilus shell grows in a logarithmic spiral closely related to the golden ratio, sometimes called the "golden spiral."

Flower Petals

Most flowers have a Fibonacci number of petals: lilies (3), buttercups (5), delphiniums (8), marigolds (13), daisies (21, 34, 55, or 89).

DNA Molecule

A DNA molecule measures 34 angstroms long by 21 angstroms wide for each full cycle of its double helix. Both 34 and 21 are Fibonacci numbers.

Galaxies

Spiral galaxies like the Milky Way have arms that follow logarithmic spirals with proportions linked to the golden ratio.

Rabbit Problem

Leonardo of Pisa (Fibonacci) originally described the sequence in 1202 as a model for rabbit population growth under idealized conditions.

Music

A piano octave has 13 keys: 8 white and 5 black. The musical scale is based on 8 notes. All Fibonacci numbers!
